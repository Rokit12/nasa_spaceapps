Van Allen Probes

NASA’s Van Allen Probes study the physical dynamics of the radiation belts that comes from solar wind captured by the Earth. Radio waves or Very Low Frequency waves can travel through water with ease for submarine communication. These radios spread outwards, enclosing the earth in a bubble, which provides a shield from the nearby radiation belt.

Juno discovers volcanic eruption

Juno discovers volcanic eruption in Jupiter’s Moon Io. Its volcanoes release one ton of material into space. This material splits up into ions and electrons to be captured by Jupiter’s magnetic field. As Io sweeps past Jupiter, Io’s raining electrons generate the powerful radio waves of Jupiter, which remained a mystery until this mission.

TRACE mission

TRACE mission explores the diffusion of magnetic fields on the solar surface. TRACE data allowed us to determine the nature of local magnetic restructurings. By imaging an entire cycle for the first time, this mission observed a “solar moss” found at the base of coronal loops. These coronal loops become solar eruptions or coronal mass ejections that are captured by the Earth’s magnetic field. This constitutes the Van Allen radiation belts.

From these data,

We can understand how the sun affects the solar system, as well as the radio waves that seem to permeate through space.

Apollo 11

Apollo 11 was the spaceflight that first landed humans on the Moon. Studying morning rocks/regolith (which were collected in the Apollo 11 mission) NASA scientists found a lot of Anorthosite. Anorthosites are formed by the crystallization of minerals at very high temperatures.

Jet Propulsion Laboratory

The Jet Propulsion laboratory mainly focuses on robots. However, it also studies earthquakes and the earth’s composition. Analyzing the moon rocks, scientists found an abundance of oxygen isotopes as in rocks on earth. It was strangely similar to the earth's crust/upper layer.




This leads us to a possible collision between earth and another celestial object. After which, a part of the earth was separated. That produced high heat and energy, thus triggering crystallization. Keep in mind that Anorthosite generally forms by crystallization. Since it is less dense than magma, it is thus found on the surface or the crust.

Same isotopic abundance is the huge clue in this topic. The Curiosity Rover studied Asteroids and Mars meteorites. The isotopic abundance there is quite different there from the Earth's and Moon's.

Cosmic Microwave Background

There are studies of different types of Electromagnetic Radiation to understand the universe. The Cosmic Background Explorer, also known as COBE, studies the oldest light ever, which is CMB or Cosmic Microwave Background. It took precise measurements of the radiation. However, this CMB was only formed 380000 years after the Big Bang, leaving a huge gap in our understanding of the earliest universe.

The LISA Pathfinder

The LISA Pathfinder tests technology for future missions to detect gravitational waves with a space-based observatory. The first light or CMB was formed after 300 millennia since the Big Bang, but there should also be a gravitational wave that we can detect from this event.

In other words, scientists can also look for a Gravitational-Wave Background. This background would have occurred within the first second of the big bang so it would give us information that is almost as old as the big bang. It could reveal the secrets to the creation of the universe.

The SMAP mission

The SMAP mission is to study soil moisture on earth to improve studies on agriculture and food security, while also providing early warnings for droughts. It can assist crop production and improve crop yield forecasts and irrigation planning around the world. This will target food security by also forecasting the weather, continuously observing the state of the atmosphere, and including the level of moisture of the ground soil.

Isolated Spaceflight Simulation

NASA has selected crew members for an isolated spaceflight simulation study. By studying crew interactions, health, and physiology during confinement, NASA researchers will advance the development of methods and technologies to mitigate problems on future spaceflight missions.

With NASA’s ongoing human research, spaceflight simulation can help us to have an indirect experience of other-worldly things like weaker gravity. These new insights are key to expanding the scope of humankind.

Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter

Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter’s primary goals are to map the Martian surface with a high-resolution camera to select sites for future landings. It also studies have included the Martian climate, weather, atmosphere, and geology. In other words, it is observing the atmospheric conditions of Mars. Therefore, it can also potentially assess how Mars’s weaker gravity affects the climate around the planet, and what this means for Martian agriculture.
